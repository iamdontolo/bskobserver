Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you might want to know about our newsroom.

Is BSK Observer really independent?

Yes. We take no government funding and accept no political party financing. Our journalism is protected by a strict editorial firewall, and every editorial decision is made solely on the basis of journalistic merit, accuracy, and public interest.

Who owns BSK Observer?

BSK Observer is a publication of BSK TV Network, part of the BK & BSK Group of Companies — founded and led by Dr. Bernadette Sanko Koroma. The Group operates across Maryland, USA and Freetown, Sierra Leone.

How do I send a confidential news tip?

Email tips@bskobserver.com or use our WhatsApp Tips Line at +232 [NUMBER]. For sensitive investigations, contact investigations@bskobserver.com — SecureDrop is available on our Submit a Story page.

Do you accept guest opinion pieces?

Yes. Send submissions to letters@bskobserver.com. All opinion pieces are reviewed for factual accuracy but the views expressed remain those of the author.

How do you handle corrections?

Every correction is published promptly, clearly and without excuse. See our full Editorial Policy for details.

How can I support your journalism?

Read our stories, share them, subscribe to the Daily Briefing, donate, or volunteer as a citizen journalist or community correspondent.

Do you cover the Sierra Leonean diaspora?

Yes — our London and Washington/Maryland desks produce dedicated diaspora coverage, and we welcome tips from Sierra Leoneans anywhere in the world.

Can I advertise on BSK Observer?

Yes. Visit our Advertise page or email ads@bskobserver.com. Advertising has no influence on our editorial coverage.
