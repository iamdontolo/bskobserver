About BSK Observer

Independent. Investigative. Accountable. Fearless.

"We do not publish what is convenient. We publish what is true. And we will never stop." — BSK Observer Editorial Charter

Who We Are

BSK Observer is Sierra Leone's premier independent digital newspaper and multimedia news organization. We are not a government mouthpiece. We are not a partisan publication. We are not for sale to the highest bidder. We are a newsroom staffed by professional journalists, editors, photographers, videographers, and digital media specialists whose singular purpose is to serve the public interest.

We are the digital arm of BSK TV Network, part of the BK & BSK Group of Companies, an international organization operating across Maryland, USA and Freetown, Sierra Leone, led by Dr. Bernadette Sanko Koroma, PhD, EdD, DMin, MS, BA, Global Chancellor, President & CEO.

Our Mission

To deliver fearless, fact-based, independent journalism that empowers Sierra Leoneans to participate fully in the democratic life of their nation and their world.

Our mission is grounded in five pillars: Truth, Independence, Community Service, Innovation, and Impact.

Our Vision

A Sierra Leone in which citizens have access to reliable, independent information; where journalists can work freely and safely; where public institutions are transparent and accountable; and where every community — from Freetown to the most remote district — is represented in the national conversation.

Editorial Independence

BSK Observer operates under a strict editorial firewall between our journalism and our business operations. Advertisers have no influence over our editorial decisions. No government body, political party, private corporation, or individual donor — regardless of the size of their financial contribution — has the right to dictate, influence, or suppress our journalism.

Our Commitments To You

  1. We will pursue truth with rigor, courage, and humility.
  2. We will be transparent about our methods, our funding, and our editorial decisions.
  3. We will correct our errors promptly, clearly, and without excuse.
  4. We will protect the identity and safety of sources who speak to us at personal risk.
  5. We will give voice to those who have been silenced, marginalized, or overlooked.
  6. We will hold power accountable — no matter whose power it is.
  7. We will never allow commercial, political, or personal interests to compromise our journalism.
  8. We will serve our communities with empathy, respect, and cultural understanding.
  9. We will reflect the diversity of Sierra Leone in our coverage and in our team.
  10. We will remain free — for every reader, in every community, forever.
